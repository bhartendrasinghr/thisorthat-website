The part nobody tells you
Prepaying is a guaranteed 8.5% return, tax-free.

Every rupee you put on the principal saves you the loan's interest rate for the rest of the tenure. No market can promise you that. To beat it, an investment would need to earn more than 8.5% after tax, every year, with zero risk. That is a high bar.

The honest fork
So, prepay or invest?
Prepay wins when
  • Your rate is high, or you are not claiming the interest deduction.
  • You value certainty and a lighter mind over a maybe-higher return.
  • You already have an emergency fund and your goals are on track.
Investing may win when
  • Your loan rate is low and you fully use Sec 24(b) and 80C breaks.
  • Your horizon is long and you can stay invested through the falls.
  • You need to keep the cash liquid for goals or safety.

A common middle path: keep an emergency fund and your long-term SIPs running, and prepay with whatever is genuinely surplus. This is education, not advice; the right call depends on your full picture.

Why prepayment is so powerful
In the early years, almost all of your EMI is interest.

On a fresh 20-year loan, the first EMI can be more than 90% interest and barely any principal. That is why the balance seems stuck for years. A prepayment skips the queue: it goes 100% to principal, so it erases the interest that principal would have earned the bank for the entire rest of the loan.

That is also why prepaying early is worth far more than prepaying late. The same lump sum in year 2 kills a decade of interest; in year 15 it barely moves the needle. If you are going to do it, sooner beats bigger.
