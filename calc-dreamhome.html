The 3 · 20 · 30 · 40 rule

A home you can afford, not just one you qualify for.

Ghar lena hai, phasna nahi.

A bank will happily approve a loan that quietly eats your life, then leave you to live in whatever is left over. The old 3/20/30/40 rule draws a kinder line: a home up to 3× your income, repaid over 20 years, with the EMI under 30% of your monthly pay and 40% down. Put in your income and see what that actually buys, whether the EMI leaves you room to breathe, and the down payment nobody warns you about.

The 3/20/30/40 rule is a guideline, not a law; your right number depends on job stability, other goals, existing loans, and local property prices, which vary hugely across India. EMIs assume a fixed rate; real home-loan rates float and change. Registration, stamp duty, GST, society and maintenance costs are extra.
