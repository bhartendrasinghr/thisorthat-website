Fund manager. Explains why SIP and equity investing remain misunderstood by most retail investors.

## In their own words

> A SIP is only the pipe, not the investment: know the fund before you commit.

## What they care about

Sneha Jain, a fund manager and CEO of Wealth Trust, keeps pulling investors back to one distinction: the method is not the investment. A SIP is just the pipe, so what matters is the fund underneath it, and on equity she argues for mutual funds as the base of a portfolio, around 60 to 65 percent, with direct stocks kept as a smaller, disciplined satellite you actually have time to research.

On retirement she is just as plain-spoken, asking you to face the real cost early, size your corpus against inflation and healthcare rather than today's expenses, and review the plan as life changes. A listener leaves knowing what to own, why starting early is the edge you cannot buy back, and how to keep a plan honest over time.
