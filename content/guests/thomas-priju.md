## In their own words

> Judge a fund manager over five years, not a couple of underwhelming quarters.

## What they care about

Thomas Priju is a fund manager with more than 25 years across mutual funds, PMS, hedge funds and AIFs, and he talks about how managers actually think rather than how they pitch. His recurring themes are the honest ones: alpha is the real engine of returns, but stock picking is genuinely hard, which is why sector cycles and business inflection points carry so much weight, and why an oversubscribed IPO of a loss-making company should make you pause.

The takeaway for a listener is patience over reaction: give a manager five years rather than a few quarters, and weigh process, humility and temperament more than any single clever call.
