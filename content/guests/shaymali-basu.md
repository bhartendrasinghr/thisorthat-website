Financial planner focused on the emotional side of wealth-building, why most investing decisions are about psychology, not math.

## In their own words

> Your own fear, greed and FOMO do more damage to your wealth than any market move.

## What they care about

Shaymali Basu spent more than 30 years at HDFC Mutual Fund, and she keeps returning to one idea: the biggest threat to your wealth is not the market, it is your own FOMO, herd mentality, fear and greed. She makes the case for goal-based investing, where every rupee is tied to a purpose, over trying to time the market, and for letting your asset allocation shift as you move through your 20s, 30s and 40s.

Her cautions are practical. Over-diversification and investing at random quietly hurt you, so a focused portfolio you actually review beats a scattered one you forget, and starting late is not a dead end when you build the saving habit and attach real goals to your money.
