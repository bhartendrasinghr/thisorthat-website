Founder of Credence Wealth and one of India's most-followed CFPs. Guest on This or That on global investing and freedom-through-wealth.

## In their own words

> For 99% of investors, global investing is FOMO, not strategy; a disciplined India portfolio does the work.

## What they care about

Kirtan Shah, founder of Credence Wealth, treats financial planning as far more than picking investments: it starts with managing risk, an emergency fund and taxes, and only then builds the portfolio. He is blunt that most of the online global-investing buzz is FOMO, and argues that for the vast majority of retail investors a disciplined, India-focused allocation is still the sensible path.

His recurring point is that asset allocation is behavioral management meant to keep you steady, not just cap risk, and that your plan should shift with your life stage. A listener leaves with a calmer checklist: buy term insurance, keep investing toward clear goals, resist chasing last year's winner, and remember to actually enjoy the life you are saving for.
