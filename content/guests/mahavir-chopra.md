Founder of Beshak.org. Speaks on health and life insurance, why most people are mis-sold both.

## In their own words

> A policy should protect you, not invest for you, so never let one product quietly try to do both.

## What they care about

Mahavir Chopra, founder of Beshak.org, wants Indians to buy insurance for one job: protection. Across his episodes he keeps protection and investing apart, favouring plain term cover over bundled ULIPs and endowments, and pushing you to own a health policy that stays with you through a job change, a layoff or a diagnosis rather than leaning on corporate cover.

He is unusually practical about the fine print: fill the proposal form honestly because it is legally binding, and judge an insurer by its complaints ratio and network hospitals, not the claim settlement ratio alone. A listener leaves seeing insurance as risk cover rather than a savings scheme, and knowing the smart choices are the careful ones made at the buying stage.
