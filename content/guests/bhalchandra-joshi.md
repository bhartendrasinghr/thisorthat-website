Three decades of mutual fund experience. On the show with the unsexy lesson nobody wants to hear: SIP works. He's seen it. The data backs it. The only thing that goes wrong is the investor.

## In their own words

> The cycle you can't sit through is the cycle you can't profit from.

## What they care about

Bhalchandra Joshi has spent more than thirty years building India's mutual fund industry, and as COO of The Wealth Company he speaks from what he has lived through rather than theory: real crashes, real bull runs, real investor panic. His case is plain: SIPs keep working, and the hard part is not starting one but continuing it when markets fall.

He watches investors repeat the same mistakes at each correction, and points out that even seasoned experts lean on an advisor, so going it alone is not automatically the smarter route. The takeaway is quieter than any tip: patience and a sensible number of funds, not constant activity, are what build wealth over decades.
