## In their own words

> Investing is simple to describe but hard to live: your behaviour, not the market, builds your wealth.

## What they care about

Mohit Bhatia keeps returning to one uncomfortable truth: the hard part of investing is not knowing the rules, it is living by them. He separates an information edge, an analytical edge and a behavioural edge, and argues the behavioural one decides most outcomes, since investor returns rarely match market returns.

A listener leaves with practical framing: sensible diversification beats owning 10 to 20 random funds, asset allocation plus tools like SIP, STP and SWP can turn volatility into a friend, and a good advisor earns their value mostly by managing behaviour and simplifying choices.
