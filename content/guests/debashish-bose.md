Founder of Infinite Circle. Brings a global macro lens to Indian markets, covers cross-border investing, bond-equity dynamics, and dollar flows.

## In their own words

> A stuck feeling is not the same as a broken plan, valuations simply have to compress first.

## What they care about

Debashish Bose founded Infinite Circle after stepping away from managing money in 2024, when a roughly fourfold jump in shareholder counts convinced him IPOs had become a tool for distribution rather than value creation. He argues India's market cap sitting near twice its GDP is hard to justify, and that valuations have to compress, sometimes for years, before the next real leg up.

For a listener whose portfolio has felt stuck for months, his point is that a stuck feeling is not the same as a broken plan. He ties the local picture to a wider story of debt behind tariffs and wars and a derivatives market roughly twenty times GDP, then lands on the practical question of what sensible asset allocation looks like in 2026.
