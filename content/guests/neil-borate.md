## In their own words

> India is 4% of global markets, so an all-India portfolio isn't diversification, it's a concentrated bet.

## What they care about

Neil Borate, Deputy Editor at Mint, makes the case that investing is simpler than the industry suggests: for about 90% of people a plain core-satellite mix of mutual funds is enough, and PMS, AIF and 20% return dreams are mostly distractions. His steady refrain is that behaviour, not the product you pick, decides how you actually do.

Where he pushes further is global investing: India is roughly 4% of world markets, so an India-only portfolio sits out whole engines of profit like AI, defence and commodities. A listener leaves knowing to keep it simple while building, and once the India base is serious, to weigh a global allocation with clear eyes about the routes and costs, from LRS to Gift City.
