Veteran asset manager and founder of Vijai Mantri Financial Services. Recurring guest on This or That with sharp views on Indian markets, family businesses, and behavioural investing.

## In their own words

> If you can hold for seven years, equity is the answer; if you can't, it isn't the question.

## What they care about

Vijai Mantri, founder of VMFS, keeps returning to one uncomfortable truth: the return a fund earns and the return an investor keeps are two different numbers, and behaviour is what widens or closes the gap. He points out that Indians will hold gold for 30 years yet sell equity within three, and that impatience, not a lack of intelligence, is what quietly breaks compounding.

He reframes a falling NAV as a SIP investor's friend, favours productive assets over a metal sitting in a locker, and says a real advisor leads with risk and mentions returns last. The takeaway is less about picking cleverer funds and more about building a process you can actually hold through the noise.
