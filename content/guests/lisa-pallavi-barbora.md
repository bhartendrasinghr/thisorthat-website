Financial journalist with a special interest in women and money. On the show on taking financial charge.

## In their own words

> Build awareness of your own numbers first, and every money conversation after that gets more honest.

## What they care about

Lisa Pallavi Barbora, a financial coach and author of Money and Her, argues that women's hesitation with money is rarely about ability: it is fear of numbers, guilt, and the weight of family responsibility, often layered with self-doubt about whether they can even learn it. Her fix is plain and doable: know your own five simple numbers first, use the 50-30-20 rule as a starting frame, and secure the foundations of an emergency fund, health insurance, and term life before shifting from saving to investing.

The takeaway a listener leaves with is that taking charge begins with a mindset shift and a willingness to learn, and that understanding your own numbers is what makes money talks with a partner easier and more honest.
