Open any mutual fund factsheet and you'll see two numbers: the fund's return, and the investor's return. The investor's return is almost always lower. Sometimes by a little. Often by a lot.

This isn't because the funds are lying. It's because investors enter at the wrong time, exit at the wrong time, and pause SIPs exactly when they should be doubling down.

## The 7% gap

Across the last 15 years, the average Indian equity mutual fund has delivered ~13% CAGR. The average investor in those funds has earned ~7%. That 6-point gap isn't fees. It isn't tax. It's behaviour.

## What to do about it

Three things, in order of impact:

1. **Automate everything.** If your SIP requires a monthly decision, you'll eventually make the wrong one.
2. **Never check NAV during a fall.** Looking is the first step toward selling.
3. **Pre-commit your response to a crash.** Write down, today, what you'll do if your portfolio drops 30%. That note is worth more than any fund selection.

The fund manager's job is to deliver the fund's return. Yours is to actually receive it.
