The part everyone skips
Rebalance once a year.

Markets will drift your mix away from these numbers. Once a year, sell a little of whatever grew too big and top up whatever shrank, back to the split above. Boring, mechanical, and it quietly forces you to sell high and buy low.

Why we ask two kinds of question

Capacity is math. Temperament is you.

1

Risk capacity

How much risk your situation can absorb. A long horizon, a steady income and an emergency fund all mean you can sit through a crash without being forced to sell. Short horizon or shaky income means you cannot, whatever your nerves say.

2

Risk temperament

How much risk you can stomach. The best allocation on paper is useless if you panic-sell at the bottom. If a 25% fall would make you bail, your mix should hold less equity, so you never reach that point.

The rule: you take the lower of the two. A brave investor with money needed in two years still cannot put it in equity. A calm long-term investor who panics in every dip should not hold 80% stocks. The mix that survives contact with a real market is the one you can actually stick to.
