The 3-Bucket Strategy.

When your corpus is one big pot, a market dip tempts anyone to sell at the bottom. That's not weakness, it's just how one pot feels. So let's split it the way seasoned planners do: 2 to 3 years in cash, 5 to 7 years in debt, the rest in equity. That way you can ride out crashes without ever having to sell.

How the 3 buckets work

You withdraw monthly from Bucket 1. Each year, Bucket 2 tops up Bucket 1. When equity has a good year, Bucket 3 tops up Bucket 2. When markets crash, you leave Bucket 3 alone and let it recover, you're still spending from cash. In years where hobby/part-time income exceeds your expenses, the surplus is invested back into Bucket 3.

Why buckets beat one big pot
-

Picture everything in equity, and the market drops 30% in your first year. You'd be forced to sell at the bottom just to cover the month, locking in losses you can't undo. The three-bucket setup exists so you're never put in that spot.

It buys you 8 to 10 years of patience, and markets have historically recovered well within that window. Your equity gets the time to do its job, and you get to sleep at night while it does.
