The one piece of money math you should know cold

How fast does your money double?

Take 72. Divide by your return rate. That's the number of years your money takes to double. It's the whole show. Once you know it, every "8% sounds safe, 15% sounds risky" conversation becomes about something concrete: how many doublings you'll see in your lifetime.

The math, in 10 seconds

Years to double = 72 ÷ rate. That's it. It's an approximation of the actual compound formula, accurate to within a few months at typical rates. At 6%, money doubles in ≈12 years. At 12%, it doubles in ≈6 years. Doubling twice as fast means three times as many doublings in a 30-year career — which is the difference between ending with ₹X and ending with ₹8X.

The uncomfortable bit
—

The Rule of 72 reframes every financial decision you'll ever make. "Should I move from FD to equity?" becomes "do I want my money doubling in 12 years, or in 6?" "Is this loan at 18% bad?" becomes "yes, because it doubles what I owe in 4 years."

The number you pick today decides how many times your wealth doubles in your lifetime. Most people only get 2-3 doublings. With the right rate started early, you can get 5-6.
