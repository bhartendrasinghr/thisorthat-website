Know Your Customer, decoded

Your mutual fund KYC, in plain English.

Before anyone can invest in a mutual fund, they have to clear a one-time identity check called KYC. Do it once, invest anywhere. Here's how to check where you stand, and exactly what each status means, so you never have to guess.

First, what even is KYC?

A one-time security check for your money.

KYC stands for Know Your Customer. It's how a mutual fund confirms you are who you say you are, using your PAN, Aadhaar and a few basic details. SEBI requires it once, before your first investment.

Think of it like the security check at an airport: clear it once, and you can board any flight. Most people do their KYC, forget about it, and never think of it again, until a status says otherwise. This page is here for exactly that moment.

Two minutes, from your phone

Check your status in 4 steps.

1
Go to any mutual fund or Registrar & Transfer Agent (RTA) website where you already invest, or a KRA site like CVL KRA or CAMS KRA.
2
Look for the "KYC Status" link.
3
Enter your 10-digit PAN.
4
Your status shows up: Validated, Registered, On-Hold or Rejected.

The part everyone gets confused by

What your status actually means.

Find yours below. Each one tells you exactly what you can do, and what (if anything) you need to fix.

✅
Validated
You're completely done.
  • • Nothing to do. Relax.
  • • You can invest in any mutual fund, any fund house, anytime, no extra paperwork ever.
🔵
Registered
Good to go, with one small catch.
  • • You can keep transacting freely in every fund you already own, purchases, redemptions, switches, SIPs, no hassle.
  • • The one catch: to invest in a new fund house you've never used before, you'll need to validate your KYC first.
  • • The good news: upgrading to Validated is a quick online step (see below). Once Validated, you can invest anywhere with no re-KYC ever again.
🟠
On-Hold
One small thing needs fixing.
  • • Your KYC is paused for a fixable reason, usually: mobile or email not verified, PAN not linked with Aadhaar, or a small document gap.
  • • The fund/RTA website shows you the exact reason.
  • • Fix that one thing, and your status moves to Registered or Validated, and you're transacting again.
🔴
Rejected
Needs a correction before you can transact.
  • • Same idea as On-Hold, but the check didn't pass, often a document or detail mismatch.
  • • The website tells you why. Follow the steps it shows to remediate it.
  • • Once fixed, your status becomes Registered or Validated, and you're all set to invest.
Registered → Validated

Upgrade to Validated in a few minutes.

On the fund or RTA site, use the KYC Update / Modification option and verify your identity with your PAN + Aadhaar through any one of these:

DigiLocker
m-Aadhaar app
Aadhaar XML

Once it's done, your status becomes Validated, and you can invest across every fund house with no re-KYC, ever.

If you're On-Hold or Rejected

Common reasons, and the fix.

The reason shown
What to do
Mobile or email not validated
Verify your mobile/email using the link the fund or RTA sends you.
PAN not linked with Aadhaar
Link your PAN and Aadhaar on the Income-Tax e-filing portal, then re-check your status.
Document deficiency or mismatch
Re-submit the correct document exactly as the website prompts.
Don't stress

Sorting out KYC is genuinely quick, and free.

Almost every KYC issue can be fixed from home in a few minutes. Stuck? Contact your mutual fund distributor, or the fund house's office or helpline, listed on their website.
